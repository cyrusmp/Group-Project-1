Facts About Climate Change

Fact #1: Earth's temperature has risen by about 2℉ since 1850

According to an article by Science & Information For A Climate-Smart Nation, the planet's average surface temperatures has risen by 0.11℉ per decade since 1850. The graph below depicts cooler-than-average years represented in blue and warmer-than-average years represented in red.

Fact #2: Climate change destabilizes ecosystems

Environmental factors that are impacted by climate change include:

  • temperature
  • precipitation patterns
  • ocean acidity
  • sea levels

These impacts cause shifts in species population which can lead to an ecosystem collapsing. The following photo is an example of a collapsed ecosystem, it is a comparison of the Aral Sea taken 25 years apart (in 1989 and 2014). An ecosystem collapse does not mean a complete disappearence of life from the region but rather a change in the original ecosystems characteristics.
