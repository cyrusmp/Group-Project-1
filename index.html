Facts About Climate Change

Fact #1: Earth's temperature has risen by about 2℉ since 1850

According to the Science & Information For A Climate-Smart Nation, the planet's average surface temperatures has risen by 0.11℉ per decade since 1850.
The graph below depicts cooler-than-average years represented in blue and warmer-than-average years represented in red.

Fact #2: Climate change destabilizes ecosystems

Environmental factors that are impacted by climate change include:

  • temperature
  • precipitation patterns
  • ocean acidity
  • sea levels

These shifts disrupt an ecosystems natural balance creating a domino effect of changes that can lead to the eventual collapse of an ecosystem.
The following photo is an example of a collapsed ecosystem, it is a comparison of the Aral Sea taken 25 years apart (in 1989 and 2014). An ecosystem collapse does not mean a complete disappearence of life from the region but rather a change in the original ecosystems characteristics.

Fact #3: Glaciers are melting at a rapid rate

Melting glaciers in Anarctica and Greenland lead to coastal erosion and elevate storm surges.
The World Wildlife Fund states that "Even if we significantly curb emissions in the coming decades, more than a third of the world's remaining glaciers will melt before the year 2100.", adding that the Arctic will be without ice as soon as 2040.

Fact #4: Climate change leads to more frequent weather events

Nasa states that heat waves, floods, droughts, wildfires, and hurricases are becoming more frequent and intense.
As air and ocean temperature warms, this affects land ice, the water cycle, and shifts weather patterns making extreme weather worse.

Fact #5: At least 10,967 species face extinction

The International Union for Conservation of Nature reported that 10,967 species are already affected by climate change and risk extinction.
In general, up to 1 million plant and animal species will be affected if current greenhouse gas emissions continue.

Fact #6: Carbon dioxide accounts for 64% of the warming effect on the climate

Carbon dioxide is the most important greenhouse gas in the atmosphere
It is created mostly through fossil fuel combustion and the production of cement according to the World Meteorological Organization.
